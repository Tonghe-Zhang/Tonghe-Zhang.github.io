Research

Focus. My research centers on the intersection of robotics and machine learning, aiming to enable robots to learn efficiently from prior knowledge and trial-and-error. I strive to develop intelligent, reliable, and cost-effective robotic systems.

Methodology. I develop algorithms based on decision-making theory, reinforcement learning (RL), imitation learning, and generative modeling. I build and analyze robotic systems drawing on my expertise in control, mechatronics, and computer science.

Philosophy. With experience in both theoretical and empirical research, I am committed to making theory applicable to real-world robotics and ensuring that experiments are well-grounded and reproducible.

Vision. My work is driven by scientific curiosity and also practical market needs. I aim to develop economically reasonable solutions for mass deployment in the future.
