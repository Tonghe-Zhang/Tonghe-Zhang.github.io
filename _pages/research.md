### Reinforcement Learning Theory

**Duration:** July 2023 - Feb 2024    **Advisor:** Longbo Huang, Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences

**Brief Introduction:**
> We present a novel formulation of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) in partially observable environments with hindsight observations. This work addresses gaps in theoretical investigation and applies to various scenarios. Key contributions include:
>
> - Formulating a risk-sensitive RL problem in partially observable Markov Decision Processes (POMDPs), incorporating non-stationary transition kernels and non-linear objectives.
> - Developing a model-based, UCB-style algorithm with a risk-sensitive bonus using imperfect state information.
> - Conducting a sample complexity analysis with a change-of-measure technique, introducing the “beta vector” for simplified derivations and comparing our regret bounds with existing results.
>
> We demonstrate that our first provably sample-efficient algorithm outperforms or matches existing bounds in simpler settings. Findings are validated through numerical experiments, and is accepted by ICML 2024.
